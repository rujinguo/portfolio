GEO Optimization for AI Search
Audited Rooms To Go’s visibility in Perplexity, Bing Copilot, and Google SGE to improve AI search presence.
---

## 🔍 Project Overview

This project aimed to understand how **Rooms To Go** appears in AI-powered search engines like **Perplexity.ai**, **Bing Copilot**, and **Google 
SGE**, and to improve our generative engine visibility.

Key objectives:
- Audit citations in AI-generated search responses
- Identify structured data gaps on key pages
- Propose visibility-enhancing content and schema changes

## 🛠 Methods & Tools

- Queried AI engines with brand + product + geo modifiers
- Logged and categorized citation behavior
- Used GA4 and GSC to analyze AI referral traffic
- Ran schema audits using Screaming Frog & Rich Results Test
- Developed a scoring framework for page visibility likelihood

## 💡 Key Findings

- **Citation gaps** even when Rooms To Go ranked highly on traditional SERPs
- Competitors with complete Product/Review schema were cited more often
- Educational/FAQ-style content was preferred by AI engines
- Some referral traffic from Perplexity was misclassified as “Direct”

## 🚀 Outcomes

- Flagged 60+ product and store pages with schema issues
- Proposed new structured data elements: `hasMap`, `reviewCount`, `availability`
- Built a framework to **simulate AI visibility score**
- Started development of a GEO monitoring dashboard

## 📌 Next Steps

- Implement schema changes across product pages
- Create content blocks (FAQs, store reviews) optimized for generative models
- Monitor visibility monthly and tag AI referrals separately

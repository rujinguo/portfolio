GEO Optimization for AI Search

Boosting Rooms To Go’s visibility in generative AI engines like ChatGPT, Bing Copilot, and Perplexity

🔍 Project Overview

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) improves how a source site is cited inside answers produced by generative engines (GEs). Instead of chasing traditional rankings, GEO optimizes the content itself so it is more quotable, citable, and extractable. At Rooms To Go (RTG), I adapted this framework to benchmark our visibility and ship content guidelines that raise our chances of being cited.

📚 Research Background (KDD 2024)

The GEO framework formalizes GE visibility as a black-box optimization problem—input: original page content; output: revised content that earns more (and higher-ranked) citations in GE responses. The authors report ~20–40% lifts from targeted edits, while keyword stuffing underperforms or harms visibility.

High-impact edits (from GEO)Why this helps in GE answers
Quotation AdditionCreates liftable, verbatim snippets models can cite.
Statistics AdditionSpecific numbers raise authority and selection odds.
Cite SourcesSignals verifiability; models prefer attributed claims.
Fluency OptimizationClear phrasing reduces extraction errors.

🌟 Objectives

  • Measure RTG’s inclusion and prominence in GE answers across shopping-intent queries.
  • Identify content gaps vs. top-cited competitors and map them to GEO edit types.
  • Ship scalable edit guidelines + schema upgrades to raise citation rate.
  • Stand up repeatable tracking for longitudinal GE visibility.

🛠 Methods

Phase 1 — Audit & Benchmark

  • Built a query set reflecting real shopping intents (price, style, material, space constraints).
  • Logged engine, prompt, answer snippet, inclusion (cited/not), position (rank), and salience (how much of our text is quoted/summarized).
  • Profiled frequently cited competitors (Wayfair, IKEA, The Spruce, Target, etc.).

Phase 2 — Edit Mapping & Content Guidelines

GEO editRTG guidelineExample application
Quotation AdditionAdd a short, liftable “Key takeaway” at top of page.“Fits 12’×16’ rooms; chaise depth 62”, 2 USB ports.”
Statistics AdditionMake specs explicit and consistent.“Top-grain leather; 3-yr protection; 4.8★ across 1,240 reviews.”
Cite SourcesAttribute standards/certifications where relevant.“Foam meets CertiPUR-US® standards.”
Fluency OptimizationLead with the shopper’s intent; cut filler.“Best sectional under $1500: …”
Technical TermsUse precise feature language.“Dual-power recline, wall-hugger design, lay-flat mechanism.”

🚀 Recommendations

  1. Make pages quotable (answer-first).
    • Add a 1–2 sentence “Key Takeaway” at the top of each product/category page with the main value prop, price/size guardrails, and 1–2 standout specs.
    • Use short, liftable lines (supports GEO’s Quotation Addition).
  2. Codify specs & stats.
    • Explicit, consistent specs: dimensions, materials, power features, warranty, review count & rating.
    • Micro-facts models can quote: “fits 12’×16’ rooms”, “chaise depth 62””, “USB-A & USB-C ports”.
  3. Intent-matching content.
    • Buying guides that mirror top intents (e.g., “Top 10 Sectionals Under $1500”, “Best Sleeper Sofas for Small Spaces”).
    • Mini-FAQs answering delivery, financing, assembly, care, space fit.
  4. Structured data that aids extraction.
    • Implement/refresh Product, Review, FAQ, LocalBusiness schema; keep synchronized.
    • Use canonical model numbers and consistent naming to reduce entity ambiguity.
  5. Local & conversion signals.
    • Strengthen store pages (inventory hints, hours, nearby availability) to win brand+geo prompts.
    • Link to /customer-service/delivery and /customer-service/financing with concise, quotable policy summaries.
  6. Measurement & iteration.
    • Track inclusion, position, and salience by query family and engine.
    • Run pre/post tests after each content release; expand query sets quarterly.
Priority rollout (6–8 weeks): Week 1–2: Spec/FAQ blocks on top 20 pages • Week 3–4: Buying-guide pair for sectionals & sleepers • Week 5–6: Schema audit & fixes • Week 7–8: Re-benchmark and report lifts.

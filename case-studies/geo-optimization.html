GEO Optimization for AI Search

Boosting Rooms To Go’s visibility in generative AI engines like ChatGPT, Bing Copilot, and Perplexity

🔍 Project Overview

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is a creator-centric, black-box approach for improving how a source site is surfaced inside answers produced by generative engines (GEs). Instead of chasing traditional web rankings, GEO optimizes the content itself so it is more quotable, citable, and extractable by GEs. At Rooms To Go (RTG), I adapted this framework to audit our current visibility, run controlled simulations, and ship content guidelines that increase our likelihood of being cited.

📚 Research Background (KDD 2024)

The GEO framework formalizes GE visibility as a black-box optimization problem—input: original page content; output: revised content that earns more (and higher-ranked) citations in GE responses. The paper introduces custom visibility metrics beyond classic SEO and shows that targeted edits can materially improve inclusion and prominence. Reported lifts are typically in the 20–40% range, with effectiveness varying by domain and query type. Tactics like keyword stuffing underperform or even hurt visibility, while edits that improve quotability, specificity, and fluency tend to help.

High-impact edits (from GEO) Why this helps in GE answers
Quotation Addition Makes content directly quotable and easier for models to lift verbatim.
Statistics Addition Concrete numbers increase specificity and authority, improving selection odds.
Cite Sources Signals verifiability; models favor content with clear attribution.
Fluency Optimization Cleaner phrasing reduces ambiguity and extraction errors.
Technical Terms (when appropriate) Aligns to feature/spec-seeking intent and reduces paraphrase loss.
Keyword Stuffing (avoid) Empirically low/negative impact; harms readability and selection.

Citation: Aggarwal, P., Murahari, V., Rajpurohit, T., Vijayakumar, A., Narasimhan, K., & Deshpande, A. (2024). GEO: Generative Engine Optimization. Proceedings of the 30th ACM SIGKDD Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (KDD ’24). DOI: 10.1145/3637528.3671900. Our methods are based on this research; we focus on edits shown to improve visibility by ~20–40%.

🌟 Objectives

  • Measure RTG’s inclusion and prominence in GE answers across shopping-intent queries.
  • Identify content gaps vs. top-cited competitors and map them to GEO edit types.
  • Ship scalable edit guidelines and schema upgrades to raise citation rate.
  • Stand up repeatable tracking for longitudinal GE visibility.

🛠 Methods

Phase 1 — Audit & Benchmark

  • Built a query set emphasizing real shopping intents (price, style, material, space constraints).
  • Logged engine, prompt, answer snippet, inclusion (cited/not), position (rank in answer), and salience (how much of our text is quoted/summarized).
  • Profiled competitors most frequently cited (Wayfair, IKEA, The Spruce, Target, etc.).

Phase 2 — Edit Mapping & Content Guidelines

GEO edit RTG guideline Example application
Quotation Addition Add short, quotable sentences summarizing the key value or spec. “This sectional fits 12’×16’ rooms; chaise depth 62”, 2 USB ports.”
Statistics Addition Insert concrete specs, dimensions, materials, warranty/ratings. “Top-grain leather; 3-yr protection; 4.8★ across 1,240 reviews.”
Cite Sources Attribute standards/tests or third-party references when relevant. “Foam meets CertiPUR-US® standards.”
Fluency Optimization Rewrite intros for clarity; front-load the answer users seek. Lead with intent (“Best sectional under $1500: …”).
Technical Terms Use precise language when shoppers seek features/specs. “Dual-power recline, wall-hugger design, lay-flat mechanism.”

🚀 Recommendations

  1. Make pages quotable (answer-first).
    • Add a 1–2 sentence “Key Takeaway” at the top of each product/category page with the main value prop, price/size guardrails, and 1–2 standout specs.
    • Use short, liftable lines (supports GEO’s “Quotation Addition”).
  2. Codify specs & stats.
    • Ensure core specs are explicit and consistent: dimensions, materials, power features, warranty, review count & rating.
    • Add micro-facts that models can quote: “fits 12’×16’ rooms”, “chaise depth 62””, “USB-A & USB-C ports”.
  3. Intent-matching content.
    • Publish buying guides that mirror the highest-volume intents (e.g., “Top 10 Sectionals Under $1500”, “Best Sleeper Sofas for Small Spaces”).
    • Add mini-FAQs per page answering the exact prompts we test (delivery, financing, assembly, care, space fit).
  4. Structured data that aids extraction.
    • Implement/refresh Product, Review, FAQ, and LocalBusiness schema. Keep values synchronized with the UI.
    • Use canonical model numbers and consistent naming to reduce entity ambiguity.
  5. Local & conversion signals.
    • Strengthen store pages with inventory hints, hours, and nearby availability to win brand+geo prompts.
    • Link clearly to /customer-service/delivery and /customer-service/financing; add concise, quotable policy summaries.
  6. Measurement & iteration.
    • Track: inclusion (cited/not), position in the answer, and salience (text quoted) by query family and engine.
    • Run pre/post tests after each content release; expand query sets quarterly as engines evolve.
Priority rollout (6–8 weeks): Week 1–2: Spec/FAQ blocks on top 20 pages • Week 3–4: Buying-guide pair for sectionals & sleepers • Week 5–6: Schema audit & fixes • Week 7–8: Re-benchmark, report lifts.

🧠 Lessons Learned

  • GEO shifts the goal from ranking to being cited inside the answer.
  • Quotability, specificity, and fluency matter more than keyword density.
  • Effectiveness varies by domain—treat GEO as an iterative, measured process.

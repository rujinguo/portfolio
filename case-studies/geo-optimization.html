🔍 Project Overview

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is an emerging content strategy focused on increasing brand visibility in AI-generated search results. At Rooms To Go, I led an internal research pilot across ChatGPT, Bing Copilot, and Perplexity, developing strategies to boost citations and traffic.

🌟 Objectives

  • Audit Rooms To Go’s visibility and citation frequency in AI responses.
  • Simulate user queries to benchmark against top competitors.
  • Identify actionable improvements to increase citation rate.
  • Build a scalable framework for ongoing AI-driven monitoring.

🛠 Methods

Phase 1: Audit Current Visibility

  • Tracked website traffic from AI engines.
  • Created high-intent queries (e.g., “best sectional sofas under $1500”).
  • Used GPT-4 to simulate results and log RTG’s citations.

Phase 2: Content & Schema Optimization

  • Selected ~20 high-traffic product pages.
  • Enhancements:
    • Real quotes & visual FAQs
    • Clear, engaging intros
    • Product, Review, FAQ & LocalBusiness schema

Phase 3: Measure Impact

  • Track citation frequency & traffic shifts
  • Compare pre/post updates

📊 Key Findings

AreaInsight
Citation GapsRTG rarely appeared in top AI citations.
Top CompetitorsWayfair, IKEA & The Spruce excel in schema & content depth.
Content WeaknessesThin descriptions, missing reviews, lack of structured answers.
SchemaLimited schema reduced AI eligibility.

🚀 Recommendations

  1. Enhance Product Pages: add quotes & FAQs
  2. Create Intent-Matching Content: blogs like “Top 10 Sectionals Under $1500”
  3. Implement Schema: Product, Review, FAQ, LocalBusiness
  4. Monitor & Iterate: dashboards for GE citations & AI traffic

🧠 Lessons Learned

  • AI prefers context-rich, human-readable content
  • Schema is critical for AI inclusion
  • Authority & fluency outweigh simple keywords
